Experience

I am a Full-Stack Software Engineer seeking to further develop my technical mastery, problem-solving and people skills through engineering software solutions using full-stack development, and/or applying cybersecurity principles. I am interested in work in the Financial Services industry in particular, where I have interned in the past and intend to intern again.

My interest in robotics led me to become involved in the Women in Engineering's all-female VEX team my freshman year, where I went on to program robots, serve as the Historian, and eventually lead the team. More information about this program can be found at the WE project teams' website . This experience holds a special place in my heart, as I made several good friends and began to see Computer Science as an option for me.

That same year, I began volunteering at the Fischer Engineering Design Center as a way to give back to the engineering community. I currently work there as a Student Technician, and have been exposed to many different sections of the makerspace. It's a thrilling place to work with lots to learn in different areas! More about the facility can be found at the Fischer Engineering Design Center's website . I primarily work in the Prototyping Center, helping students prototype concepts using a variety of methods, where I specialize in prototyping electronics and assisting students program their prototypes. Much of the powerpoint material that I made and/or modified to educate students is available in the Electronics and Programming sections of the Fischer Engineering Design Center pop-ups.

Since deciding to do software engineering, I joined Aggie Coding Club, which is dedicated to encouraging students to program through workshops on industry-standard techniques and technologies, among other activities. The workshops have introduced me to interesting topics such as basic mobile programming. More information about the organization is available at the website of the Aggie Coding Club. I also joined the Cybersecurity Club, and the weekly meetings have helped me learn more about the topic. More information about the organization can be found at Cybersecurity Club's website . My interest in Cybersecurity is being further explored through my Cybersecurity minor coursework, which includes Cybersecurity Law & Policy and Cybersecurity Risk, among other specialized classes.

These extra-curricular experiences were useful in my internship with H-E-B's San Antonio Development Center, where I got my first taste of full-stack software engineering. The process that I experienced was challenging, and rewarding; I loved it. From this experience, I know that I want to continue developing the full-stack. My next internship with American Express' Application Security team, I saw a different side of full-stack development with adding a new feature to an existing JavaScript Cybersecurity tool. The challenges of creating a tool for developers to use to improve security were interesting, and I greatly enjoyed working in the Cybersecurity space of the financial industry. Next summer, I will intern at JP Morgan & Chase. I'm not sure what I will do yet, but I'm excited to learning a different financial company's approach to technology!
