Education

George Ranch High School: Chemical Engineering Interest I've been interested in STEM for as long as I can remember, and early in high school I was most interested in science; I led the Science Olympiad club. In exchange for a space to run experiments, I made a deal with the engineering teacher that I would take the introductory engineering class. After that, I was hooked on engineering in general and planned to study Chemical Engineering at Texas A&M University.

Texas A&M University

Freshman Year: Computer Engineering Interest My freshman year of college, I joined the Women in Engineering VEX-U Robotics team to make friends with similar interests, and got quite involved on the programming side of the preparation for competition. I also took a mandatory introductory programming coursee that I extremely enjoyed. From these experiences, I realized that I enjoyed programming much more than chemistry, and I ended up majoring in Computer Engineering, a blend of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering courses instead. Sophmore and Junior Year: Computer Science Interest As I began taking Computer Science and Electrical Engineering courses, I found that I didn't enjoy and wasn't interested in being a Computer Engineer; the low-level programming and design just didn't suit me. After I completed my first internship at H-E-B as a Software Engineering Intern and experienced programming at a higher level for the first time (more about my experience is in the H-E-B Software Engineering internship section of the Work Experience page). I loved what I did there, and so I switched majors to Computer Science. Senior and Super Senior Year: Computer Science B.S.!

Since then, I've happily been studying Computer Science, and have added a Cybersecurity Minor! My interest in this has been ignited even more by my experiences in my Software Engineering internships at American Express' Application Security Engineering Team and at JP. Morgan & Chase's Corporate and Investment Banking division (more about this in the respective sections of the Work Experience page).

It has also been ignited by my experiences in Aggie Coding Club, where I learned about various Software Engineering topics, Cybersecurity Club, where I learned about Cybersecurity, and at the Grace Hopper Conferences 2018 and 2019, which gave me a great appreciation for the importance of diverse technologists and exposure to new technologies.
